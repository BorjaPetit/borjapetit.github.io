Quantitative Methods Fall 2017, Spring 2018, Spring 2019

Master/Diploma in Banking Supervision, CEMFI.
Instructor: Enrique Moral-Benito

This course revisits basic concepts in mathematics and statistics. It is structured in five topics: (i) introductory topics, (ii) fundamentals of probability, (iii) linear regression with one regressor, and (iv) multiple regression analysis. The course also provides students with an introduction to statistical analysis with STATA.

Macroeconomics II Spring 2019

Master in Economics and Finance, CEMFI.
Instructor: R. Anton Braun

This course expands the knowledge acquired in the Macroeconomics I course, extending the set of models and theories following recent developments in the discipline. In particular, the course covers: (i) competitive general equilibrium under uncertainty, (ii) fiscal policy in the basic growth model, (iii) recursive general equilibrium in stochastic productive economies with complete markets, (iv) nominal rigidities and monetary policy, and (v) heterogeneity in macroeconomics. Macroeconomics I Winter 2016, Winter 2017

Master in Economics and Finance, CEMFI.
Instructor: Josep Pijoan-Mas

This course provides the student with a basic knowledge in macroeconomics through the analysis of the neo-classical growth model and its implications for aggregate allocations in the long run. The course is divided in six parts: (i) the neoclassical growth model, (ii) multisector models, (iii) overlapping generations models, (iv) firms, (v) households, and (vi) money in the baseline growth model.
